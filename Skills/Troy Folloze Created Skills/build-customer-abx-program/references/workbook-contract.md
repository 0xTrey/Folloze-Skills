# Workbook Output Contract

## Canonical asset

Start from `../assets/Reusable_Customer_ABX_Program_Workbook.xlsx`.

## Structural requirements

- Preserve all 14 tabs and their order.
- Preserve formulas and cross-sheet references.
- Keep yellow cells editable inputs and green cells calculated outputs.
- Keep the ABX Blueprint as the executive stage-by-stage synthesis.
- Keep detailed logic in the supporting tabs.
- Add rows only when needed; do not replace tables with prose.

## Customerization rules

- Replace generic placeholders with verified customer inputs.
- Retain `[NOT PROVIDED]` and `[DECISION REQUIRED]` where evidence is missing.
- Populate the Source Files tab before finalizing strategy.
- Cite source URLs or file paths in the workbook where claims, proof, or assumptions originate.
- Never carry another customer's facts into a new workbook.

## Formula and commercial rules

- Drive calculations from Program Inputs.
- Monthly opportunity pacing must equal the annual target.
- Acquisition plus expansion must equal 100%.
- Pipeline created equals opportunity target multiplied by average opportunity value.
- Expected wins equal opportunity target multiplied by win rate.
- Expected bookings equal expected wins multiplied by average opportunity value.
- Pre-pipeline, Target, and Awareness do not count as CRM pipeline.
- Decision requires a CRM opportunity value; Purchase uses weighted pipeline or bookings.

## Visual requirements

- Purple: pillar and section identity.
- Navy: table headers.
- Pink: active engagement plays.
- Yellow: editable customer inputs and unresolved ownership/status.
- Green: formula-driven values.
- Hide gridlines and freeze useful headers.
- Wrap long content and verify every tab visually.

## Delivery QA

- No formula errors.
- No clipped headings or prompts.
- No unexplained blanks in required fields.
- No unrelated customer names.
- No unsupported performance claims.
- Source register and decision list complete.
