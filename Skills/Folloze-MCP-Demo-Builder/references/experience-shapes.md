# Experience Shapes

Choose one page shape before writing a new Folloze HTML page or materially restructuring an existing one. The shape is a working design decision, not buyer-facing language.

## Selection Rules

- The vendor's public brand and the Folloze MCP creation guide outrank the shape catalog.
- Pick the shape that best supports the buyer job, the available public proof, and the source-site DNA.
- Avoid repeating the same hero-first, three-card, final-CTA rhythm across boards unless the vendor source page clearly uses it.
- Do not choose stat-led or proof-led shapes unless the numbers, quotes, logos, or case studies are verified.
- For existing-board updates, preserve the approved structure unless the user asks for a redesign or the current page has a clear quality problem.

## Candidate Shapes

- Workbench: Guided tour of the solution in use. Best for SaaS, security, data, finance, workflow, platform, and technical products where buyers need to understand how work changes.
- Narrative Workflow: Numbered stages from current state to improved state. Best for account-specific change stories, implementation paths, operating models, and "how we help your team move" pages.
- Map Or Diagram: A spatial system map, flow, ecosystem, risk map, or operating diagram anchors the page. Best when the product connects many teams, systems, regions, or decision points.
- Bento Grid: Modular blocks of unequal size for proof, use cases, stats, resources, and account signals. Use only when the content has enough variety to avoid uniform card rows.
- Quote-Led Or Proof-Led: A verified customer quote, analyst line, customer story, or strong public proof leads the page. Do not fabricate the proof slot.
- Split Studio: Alternating text/proof panels. Best when the vendor source favors side-by-side compositions or when each claim needs a paired asset, screenshot, or account signal.
- Conversational FAQ: The page is organized around buyer objections and practical answers. Best for complex categories where skepticism, risk, compliance, or procurement friction is the main barrier.
- Photographic: Strong source-owned imagery dominates each fold. Best for retail, travel, manufacturing, health, physical product, facilities, events, or location-centered experiences.
- Long Document: Executive memo or strategic brief. Best for dense, senior-buyer narratives where clarity and credibility matter more than interactive modules.
- Ecosystem Index: Multiple browsable paths: use cases, industries, integrations, content, proof, and next actions. Best for resource-center style pages or broad platform narratives.

## Working Note Format

Before writing or revising the page, record:

```text
Experience shape:
- Shape:
- Why this shape fits the vendor and buyer motion:
- Sections in order:
- First-viewport signal:
- Nav strategy:
- Final CTA pattern:
- Proof required before use:
```

## Default Bias

- Use Workbench, Narrative Workflow, Map Or Diagram, or Split Studio for most B2B account pages.
- Use Bento Grid only when the content is genuinely modular.
- Use Quote-Led only with verified proof.
- Use Long Document when the request is closer to a strategic brief than a rich interactive page.
