# Folloze Value Props

Use this reference to map account goals and objections into Folloze one-pager copy.

Load `folloze-brand-kit` first when it is available. This file is the lightweight fallback menu and one-pager-specific pattern library, not the sole source of truth.

## Core Narrative

Folloze helps teams target and convert key accounts.

Your AI creates content. Folloze deploys it, hosts it, governs it, personalizes it, and captures the signal that drives the next move.

Use this visible structure for most one-pagers:

- Build: create personalized microsites, campaign emails, on-behalf sales emails, LinkedIn ads, and other account destinations from a brief or AI-generated content.
- Activate: deploy, schedule, segment, personalize, and route content across campaign, sales, partner, and web motions with governance applied.
- Signal: capture first-party engagement signals and show who engaged, what they consumed, where they came from, and what should move next.
- Govern: support enterprise-ready programs with brand control, security review, permissions, scalability, and repeatable launch operations.

## Value Prop Menu

| Account need or objection | Folloze framing | One-pager card idea |
|---|---|---|
| Campaigns take too long to launch | Build a governed account experience from a brief, prompt, or asset set | Faster campaign creation without rebuilding each destination |
| AI content is being created but not activated | Bring your own AI; Folloze deploys, hosts, governs, and measures the experience | Your AI creates. Folloze gets it live and captures the signal |
| Buyer journeys are too generic | Activate by account, persona, source, and stage so each visitor sees a relevant path | More relevant engagement from account and persona personalization |
| Known and unknown buyers arrive from multiple channels | Connect email, LinkedIn, website, forms, cookies, and known systems into a clearer identity path | Known buyers, partial signals, and fewer broken journeys |
| Sales needs proof, not vanity clicks | Show what buyers consumed, time spent, account activity, source path, and what should move to sales | First-party signal and sales-ready insight |
| Agency, partner, or client programs need separation | Support internal programs, client tokens, managed-service paths, and white-label/domain needs | Agency and client program routes |
| Security or procurement will slow momentum | Package enterprise readiness, SOC 2 Type 2 availability, governance, and global launch experience | Vendor review details ready for approval |
| Teams need a repeatable launch model | Standardize the build, activation, and signal path so each program starts from a proven system | Launch motion in action |
| Leadership asks how this helps pipeline | Tie engagement depth, content consumption, and account heat back to pipeline-ready follow-up | Signal every key-account journey |

## Numeric Claim Discipline

Do not introduce new metrics casually.

Allowed sources for numeric claims:

- user-approved prior one-pagers
- current Folloze sales material
- verified customer proof or public case studies
- explicit user-provided assumptions for that account

If no approved numeric claim exists, use non-numeric value language. For example, use `faster campaign creation` instead of `5x faster campaign creation`.

## Default Value Cards

The Younion-style template can start with these cards when the user has approved them for the motion:

- `5x` faster campaign creation without rebuilding each landing page
- `4-5x` more relevant buyer engagement from account and persona personalization
- `3 hours` typical build window once assets and brand are ready

Replace these when the account conversation points to stronger goals, objections, or proof needs. If the claim source is unclear, remove the number and keep the directional claim.

## Default Motion Rows

Use these as a starting point, then adapt to the account:

| Row | Title | Buyer-safe copy |
|---|---|---|
| 01 | Build | Turn AI-generated or team-generated content into personalized microsites, campaign emails, sales emails, and LinkedIn ad destinations. |
| 02 | Activate | Deploy and personalize the right content by account, source, persona, segment, and buying stage with governance applied. |
| 03 | Signal | Show who engaged, what they consumed, where they came from, and what should move to sales or the next workflow. |

Avoid visible row titles such as `Campaign Agent`, `Activation Agent`, or `Insight Agent` unless the user explicitly wants an internal-facing asset.

## Copy Rules

- Write from Folloze's point of view, but make the page about the account's next move.
- Prefer the current external language from the Brand Kit: target and convert key accounts.
- Use `Build. Activate. Signal.` for display copy and plain-language Folloze descriptions in prose.
- Use ABM as a buyer motion when relevant, not as the lead category.
- Do not say `call notes`, `meeting recap`, `demo`, `template`, or `AI-generated` in buyer-facing copy.
- Name the account's business motion when it is safe: ABM launch, website personalization, partner programs, customer expansion, event follow-up, sales enablement, or deal-room acceleration.
- Pair every objection with a Folloze support path. The page should show how Folloze helps, not simply document concerns.
