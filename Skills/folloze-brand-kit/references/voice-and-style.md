# Voice And Style

Use this file for Folloze tone, language, and copy quality checks.

## North Star

Folloze sounds like a direct, experienced peer who has been in the trenches with B2B marketers.

Not a vendor performing expertise.

The voice is confident without bravado, specific over abstract, and willing to name what is broken. It calls AI by its real name and explains what AI actually does. It meets marketers as peers, not prospects.

## Technical Accuracy

Bold writing only earns trust when it is accurate.

If a claim oversimplifies the funnel, gets the buyer journey wrong, or describes a tactic in a way that exposes shallow marketing knowledge, sophisticated practitioners will check out.

The bar is not "does it sound smart." The bar is "would a senior demand gen lead nod or roll their eyes."

When in doubt:

- name a real channel
- name a real step
- name a real constraint
- avoid compressing the funnel for drama

## Voice Pillars

1. Direct, not soft. Make claims. Do not hedge with `might`, `could be`, or `consider exploring` when the point is true.
2. Specific over abstract. A concrete scenario beats a broad persona label.
3. Peer, not vendor. Write to marketers who know their jobs.
4. AI named, not AI implied. Say Claude, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, LLM, prompt, or MCP when relevant.
5. Bold, but accurate. Provocative claims need to be defensible.

## Intensity By Format

| Intensity | Use for | Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| High | Thought leadership, LinkedIn, founder content, TOFU campaigns | Lead with a bold claim or contrarian take |
| Mid | Blog posts, nurture emails, landing pages, sales decks | Direct claims with proof and context |
| Low | Enterprise, procurement, RFP, renewal, customer communications | Same directness, less provocation |

The voice does not change. The dial moves.

## Lexicon

Use these terms deliberately.

| Term | Usage |
|---|---|
| Build, Activate, Signal | Preferred display-copy register |
| Deploy, host, govern, personalize, capture signal | Plain-language prose register |
| Microsite | External noun for the personalized digital experience Folloze builds |
| Board | Internal product term or customer term for existing users |
| Personalized account experience | Broader external descriptor when microsite is too narrow |
| Engagement signal | First-party intent data Folloze captures |
| Speed of personalization | Better than generic speed because it names what speed produces |
| Frontline marketers | Practitioners who run campaigns: demand gen, ABM, field marketing |
| Claude, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini | Use real AI tool names when relevant |
| LLM, agent, prompt, MCP | Use when the audience will understand the workflow |

## Internal-Only Terms

Use these only in internal docs, sales decks, product docs, or team conversations where the audience already knows Folloze:

- activation layer
- campaign agent
- activation agent
- insight agent

For external customer-facing content, describe what the capability does instead of naming it.

Do not use em dashes. Use commas, periods, parentheses, or restructure the sentence.

## Usage Notes

### Autonomous

Use `autonomous` only when describing a genuine autonomous workflow: automated engine mode, deal-triggered rooms, signal-triggered campaigns, or similar. If the behavior is not actually autonomous, describe the specific automation instead.

### Agentic

Use `agentic` only when the workflow is actually agent-driven. Do not use it as a vague adjective.

### Orchestration

Use `orchestration` when describing real workflow orchestration across systems. Do not use it as decoration.

### Naming

Not every feature needs a name. When a capability can be described in language the buyer already understands, use the description.

Good:

- personalized microsites
- engagement signals
- on-behalf sales emails
- deploy, host, govern, and capture signal

Avoid leading with internal labels when the reader does not know them yet.

### Category Language

Use familiar buyer language externally:

- ABM
- campaign
- microsite
- personalized experience
- engagement signal
- personalized account experience

Save invented category language for internal strategy docs.

## Before / After

Generic SaaS:

> Folloze is a leading AI-powered Buyer Experience Platform that empowers B2B marketing teams to unlock personalized engagement at scale.

Folloze voice:

> Bring your own AI to create personalized account experiences. Folloze handles deploy, host, signal capture, and governance.

Generic ad headline:

> Discover how leading enterprises are leveraging Folloze's revolutionary AI-powered platform to drive transformational pipeline outcomes.

Folloze voice:

> Personalization is the single biggest lever you have on conversion. Folloze gives you the speed and depth to pull it for every account that matters.

Generic email opener:

> I hope this email finds you well. I wanted to reach out because I noticed you might benefit from learning more about how our innovative platform could help streamline your ABM efforts.

Folloze voice:

> Saw you're hiring two more demand gen marketers. Most teams in that growth phase hit the same wall: web is the bottleneck, not headcount. Worth a 15-minute conversation?

## Sentence-Level Patterns

- Vary sentence length deliberately.
- Let most paragraphs land on a short sentence.
- Use three beats: set up, name what is true, deliver the implication.
- Keep subjects active.
- Use present tense for what is true.
- Use second person in marketing copy.
- Use first person in thought leadership when appropriate.
- Avoid third-person abstractions such as `the marketer` when writing to marketers.
- Do not use em dashes.

## Copy Quality Gate

Before finalizing a customer-facing asset, check:

- Does the copy lead with a clear buyer outcome?
- Does it use the current frame: target and convert key accounts?
- Does it show how Folloze builds, activates, or captures signal?
- Does it avoid external `activation layer` and agent-name-led messaging?
- Does it avoid ABX-first category framing?
- Are all numeric claims approved and relevant?
- Does every account-specific point trace to a source?
- Does every Folloze claim trace to approved messaging or this brand kit?
- Would a seller feel confident repeating the line in a live call?
- Would a senior demand gen lead nod or roll their eyes?
- Is the sentence shorter and simpler than the first draft?
- Did it avoid em dashes?

If any answer is no, revise before exporting or publishing.

## Writing For Follow-Up Assets

Follow-up one-pagers, recap pages, and PDFs should not read like meeting notes.

Use this shape:

1. Name the account's next business move.
2. Explain how Folloze helps that move.
3. Show the proof or governance path that addresses objections.
4. Give the seller or CSM a clear next conversation path.

Do not say:

- call notes
- recap generated from notes
- AI-generated
- internal discussion
- draft template
- demo page

Use:

- path
- plan
- workspace
- launch motion
- proof model
- approval path
- account experience
- signal path
