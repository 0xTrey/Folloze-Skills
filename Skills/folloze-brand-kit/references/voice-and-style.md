# Voice And Style

Use this file for Folloze tone, language, and copy quality checks.

## Voice

Folloze copy should be:

- direct
- clear
- action-oriented
- professional but conversational
- specific instead of abstract
- strategic for VP and C-level audiences
- grounded in current product reality

Use short sentences and active voice. Prefer buyer outcomes over feature lists.

## Tone By Audience

| Audience | Tone | Best opening angle |
|---|---|---|
| Practitioners | practical, specific, execution-focused | launch faster, remove handoffs, get more done |
| VP Revenue Marketing / CMO | strategic, concise, business-outcome focused | scale without headcount, growth momentum, pipeline visibility |
| RevOps / Marketing Ops | operational, credible, systems-aware | governance, integrations, attribution, repeatability |
| Enterprise leaders | serious, control-aware, proof-backed | remove friction without losing control |
| Sales / CS enablement | buyer-action oriented | know who engaged and what to do next |

## Phrases To Favor

- AI orchestration
- autonomous marketing with governance
- from prompt to pipeline
- campaigns that improve themselves
- revenue visibility
- orchestrate execution
- scale without headcount
- launch campaigns faster
- remove friction without losing control
- build an always-on growth engine
- engagement-based optimization
- account-level engagement analytics
- pipeline-ready insight

## Phrases To Avoid

- Buyer Experience Platform as the primary category
- ABX platform as the primary category
- revolutionary
- cutting-edge
- set it and forget it
- full autonomy
- AI replaces marketers
- generator AI
- generic transformation language
- corporate-speak phrases such as "leverage synergies" or "optimize go-to-market velocity"

## Good / Bad Examples

Good:

- Launch campaigns faster.
- One person can market like a team.
- Remove friction without losing control.
- Show who engaged, what they consumed, and what should move to sales.
- Connect campaign activity to account-level insight and pipeline-ready follow-up.

Avoid:

- Accelerate go-to-market velocity through optimized workflows.
- Resource optimization enables contributors to scale impact.
- Leverage AI-powered synergies for exponential growth.
- Implement continuous optimization paradigms.

## Copy Quality Gate

Before finalizing a customer-facing asset, check:

- Does the copy lead with a clear buyer outcome?
- Does it connect to Scale, Engage, or Impact?
- Does it avoid ABX-first category framing?
- Is autonomous marketing grounded in current product reality?
- Are all numeric claims approved and relevant?
- Does every account-specific point trace to a source?
- Does every Folloze claim trace to approved messaging or this brand kit?
- Would a seller feel confident repeating the line in a live call?
- Is the sentence shorter and simpler than the first draft?

If any answer is no, revise before exporting or publishing.

## Writing For Follow-Up Assets

Follow-up one-pagers, recap pages, and PDFs should not read like meeting notes.

Use this shape:

1. Name the account's next business move.
2. Explain how Folloze helps that move.
3. Show the proof or governance path that addresses objections.
4. Give the seller or CSM a clear next conversation path.

Do not say:

- call notes
- recap generated from notes
- AI-generated
- internal discussion
- draft template
- demo page

Use:

- path
- plan
- workspace
- launch motion
- proof model
- approval path
- campaign journey
- pipeline-ready signal
