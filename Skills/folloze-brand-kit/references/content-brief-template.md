# Content Brief Template

Use this reference when creating a new Folloze campaign asset, one-pager, landing page, sales follow-up, email, ad, social post, thought-leadership piece, or microsite copy from a loose request.

The brief keeps messaging separate from layout. Fill it before writing polished copy.

## Required Brief Fields

| Field | What to capture |
|---|---|
| Content type | One-pager, landing page, blog, email, LinkedIn post, ad, sales follow-up, board copy, slide, or other format |
| Primary persona | CMO, VP marketing, demand gen, ABM, field marketing, rev ops, marketing ops, seller, CSM, or other |
| Lead hook | The opening angle or tension |
| Funnel stage | TOFU, MOFU, BOFU, expansion, renewal, partner, or customer enablement |
| Pain point | The real problem the piece must answer |
| Audience maturity | New to Folloze, existing customer, technical buyer, AI-forward buyer, conservative enterprise buyer |

## Strategic Context

Capture these when available:

- industry or segment
- named account or customer
- region or language needs
- current campaign motion
- current stack or tools named by the account
- mode of operation: LLM-centric, headless, on-platform, automated engine, or mixed
- relationship status: prospect, customer, renewal, expansion, partner, agency, or internal

## Folloze Message Mapping

Create a compact matrix before drafting:

| Audience signal | Source | Folloze value prop | Buyer-facing claim | Proof or support |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Rules:

- The signal must come from user direction, call notes, Salesforce, Drive, Gmail/Calendar, Granola, public research, or an approved Folloze source.
- The value prop should map to Build, Activate, Signal, Govern, or a current proof pillar.
- The buyer-facing claim must be safe for the intended audience.
- Proof must be verified before using customer names or numbers.

## Message Options

Pick only the options that fit the brief.

### Core Frame

- Folloze helps teams target and convert key accounts.
- Your AI creates content. Folloze deploys, hosts, governs, personalizes, and captures signal.
- Folloze is how your content reaches your accounts. And how your accounts reach back.

### Display Register

- Build.
- Activate.
- Signal.

### Prose Pattern

Use:

> Your AI does X. Folloze does Y.

Examples:

- Your AI creates account-specific content. Folloze turns it into a live, governed microsite.
- Your AI drafts the campaign. Folloze deploys it, personalizes it, and captures the signal sales needs next.
- Your AI finds the angle. Folloze gets it in front of the account and shows who is paying attention.

## Output Requirements

Before drafting, decide:

- tone intensity: high, mid, or low
- output length
- number of variants
- required proof or proof placeholder
- allowed customer names
- required CTA
- visual or format constraints
- whether the output is external, internal, or partner-facing

## Quality Gate

Before final delivery:

- Does the piece name a real buyer problem?
- Does it connect AI creation to Folloze deployment, governance, personalization, or signal capture?
- Does it use external language for external audiences?
- Did it avoid external `activation layer` and agent-name-led messaging?
- Did it avoid banned generic SaaS phrasing?
- Are all numbers and customer claims verified or clearly marked as placeholders?
- Does it sound like a peer who understands B2B marketing work?
