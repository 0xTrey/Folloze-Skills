# Messaging Library

Use this file for approved Folloze value props, pillars, reusable copy blocks, and account-message mapping.

## Core Message

Folloze helps B2B revenue teams launch, optimize, and prove autonomous campaigns from prompt to pipeline.

Shorter variants:

- Launch, optimize, and prove campaigns from prompt to pipeline.
- Scale campaign execution without adding headcount.
- Turn buyer engagement into sales-ready insight.
- Orchestrate campaign creation, personalization, and revenue visibility in one system.

## Approved Messaging Anchors

Use these as anchor lines or inspiration:

- From prompt to pipeline.
- You orchestrate. AI executes.
- Campaigns that improve themselves.
- Scale like a team of 10 with a team of 1.
- Finally see what drives revenue.
- Launch campaigns faster.
- Remove friction without losing control.
- Build an always-on growth engine.
- Growth comes from momentum, not one-off launches.

Use "Not faster marketing. Autonomous marketing." selectively. It is useful as a campaign line, but one-pagers and follow-ups often need more concrete buyer context.

## Pillar Message Blocks

### Scale

Use when the account cares about speed, lean teams, campaign bottlenecks, agency handoffs, or doing more with current resources.

Core ideas:

- Increase campaign output without increasing headcount.
- Move from brief, prompt, or assets into launched campaign destinations faster.
- Reuse governed structures instead of rebuilding each experience.
- Keep marketing in control without waiting on web-team or dev cycles.

Copy options:

- Launch campaigns faster without rebuilding every destination from scratch.
- Give lean teams a governed workspace to create more campaign moments with the same resources.
- Move from strategy to live campaign execution with fewer handoffs.
- Scale execution while keeping brand, content, and approvals under control.

### Engage

Use when the account cares about personalization, buyer relevance, unknown visitors, content discovery, journey fragmentation, or low engagement.

Core ideas:

- Personalization should respond to behavior, account, source, persona, and stage.
- Campaigns should improve based on what buyers actually consume.
- First-party engagement should guide the next best journey and the next best sales action.

Copy options:

- Personalize by account, segment, persona, source, and stage so every visitor gets a more relevant path.
- Use engagement signals to adapt content journeys instead of forcing every buyer through the same experience.
- Turn content consumption into clearer buyer intent.
- Help known and unknown buyers find the right path faster.

### Impact

Use when the account cares about pipeline, attribution, executive reporting, campaign proof, sales alignment, or proving marketing's influence.

Core ideas:

- Engagement should connect to account insight, opportunity influence, and pipeline reporting.
- Sellers need to know who engaged, what they consumed, and what to do next.
- Marketing needs proof that digital journeys create measurable movement.

Copy options:

- Show who engaged, what they consumed, and what should move to sales.
- Connect campaign activity to account-level insight and pipeline-ready follow-up.
- Give marketing and sales a shared view of buyer heat.
- Turn engagement into a clearer bridge from campaign activity to revenue.

### Govern

Use when the account cares about enterprise readiness, security, procurement, brand control, regional rollout, localization, partner/client programs, or repeatability.

Core ideas:

- Enterprise teams need speed with control.
- Governance should be visible early enough to avoid slowing approval.
- Repeatable campaign systems need brand, access, analytics, and content discipline.

Copy options:

- Move quickly without losing enterprise control.
- Package brand, security, and governance details early so approval can move in parallel.
- Standardize campaign execution across regions, teams, partners, and client programs.
- Launch global programs with a governed foundation.

## Product Narrative

Use the agent narrative when the asset needs to explain how Folloze works.

| Product narrative | What it means | Buyer-safe copy |
|---|---|---|
| Campaign Agent | Campaign creation across destinations and assets | Turns brand guidelines, content assets, and persona logic into campaign destinations |
| Activation Agent | Personalization and signal-based activation | Personalizes journeys by account, source, segment, and known identity |
| Insight Agent | Engagement insight and revenue visibility | Shows who engaged, what they consumed, and what should move to sales |

Use "agent" language only when the audience will understand the AI orchestration frame. For conservative enterprise audiences, "workspace", "activation", and "insight" may be clearer.

## Message-Fit Matrix

Before writing page copy for account-specific assets, create this matrix:

| Account signal | Source | Folloze value prop | Buyer-facing claim | Page placement |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Rules:

- Use account signals from call notes, Salesforce, Drive, Gmail/Calendar, or public research.
- Use Folloze value props from this brand kit or approved current materials.
- Put only externally safe language in buyer-facing claims.
- Use the matrix to choose what goes into the layout.

The matrix prevents generic Folloze copy from crowding out the account's real reason to care.
