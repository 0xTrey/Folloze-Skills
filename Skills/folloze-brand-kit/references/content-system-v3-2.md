## Category Point Of View

Targeting and converting key accounts is the work. Folloze is how marketing teams do that work with the personalization and speed AI now makes possible.

The job is still to find the right accounts, deliver the right message, and win the deal. AI changes the how. Folloze is where AI-created or team-created content becomes a live, governed, measurable account experience.

Bridge line:

> Folloze is how your content reaches your accounts. And how your accounts reach back.

## Core Value Proposition

Folloze helps you target and convert your key accounts.

Bring your AI to create. Folloze deploys the content, hosts the experience, captures engagement signals, and helps every campaign learn from the last.

## Core Beliefs

1. More targeted messaging equals higher conversion.
2. AI gives teams the speed to personalize at scale when they use it well.
3. Folloze meets teams where they are.

## How AI Changes The How, Not The What

Use these four mechanics to explain the Folloze AI story:

1. Bring your own AI. Folloze is the open execution platform for Claude, ChatGPT, internal models, custom agents, and other content workflows. Folloze deploys, hosts, personalizes, governs, and captures signal.
2. Create personalized experiences in minutes. Brief in, account, industry, persona, and use-case content out. The same workflow can support microsites, campaign emails, on-behalf sales emails, ads, events, and sales rooms.
3. Capture richer first-party signals. Folloze surfaces feature interest, use-case exploration, persona context, buying-group behavior, and LLM-driven traffic that ABM platforms may miss.
4. Close the loop. Engagement data flows back into the stack so the next campaign targets proven-warm segments and personalization compounds.

## Four Modes Of Operation

These modes are not a maturity ladder. Most customers use more than one mode at the same time.

| Mode | Description | Best fit |
|---|---|---|
| LLM-centric | Work in Claude or another LLM. Folloze MCP deploys content directly with brand rules applied. | Sophisticated teams with established AI workflows |
| Stack-driven | Slack, Outreach, n8n, Salesforce, Clay, or another workflow tool triggers Folloze through API or MCP. | Marketing ops, RevOps, and sales ops teams |
| On-platform | Use Folloze directly with templates, segment rules, and board structures. | Field marketing, demand gen, and ABM teams |
| Automated engine | Signal-triggered or deal-triggered workflows build rooms, microsites, and nurture paths without manual marketer action. | Ops teams orchestrating end-to-end automation |

## On-Behalf Sales Email Activation

Use this as a differentiated activation story, especially for field-sales-led organizations.

- Marketing orchestrates the campaign.
- Sales reviews and approves the email.
- Email sends from the rep's own Outlook outbox.
- Replies go to the rep.
- Marketing gets visibility into engagement across many salespeople.

Do not position this as a replacement for SDR engagement tools. It is a way for marketing to activate field reps from Outlook, where customer relationships already live.

Status: the 2 to 3x response-rate and hundreds-of-millions pipeline claims are internal until source-verified. Mark them `[PROOF]` or keep them out of external content until approved.

| Advanced | Lead with Folloze's role in LLM workflows: deploy, host, govern, capture signal. Likely first mode: LLM-centric or stack-driven. |

## Hooks By Channel Fit

- `Break into and convert your key accounts.` Primary campaign hook for paid ads, email, and rep outreach.
- `AI broke your go-to-market playbook.` Thought-leadership hook for blog, LinkedIn, founder content, and TOFU campaigns.
- `Stop choosing between personalized and fast.` Tradeoff-killer hook for paid ads, landing pages, and sales decks.
- `3 months to launch 1 campaign? Ouch. How about an hour?` Pain-and-pivot hook for LinkedIn, email subject lines, and paid ads when paired with proof.
- `Move at the speed of AI. ABM in the AI era.` Urgency hook for top-of-funnel ads, event invites, and founder content.
- `Do more with the resources you have.` Efficiency hook for email, rep outreach, and LinkedIn.
- `What could you do with personalized content at scale?` TOFU question hook for paid ads, blog, and email.
- `Not your grandma's ABM.` Practitioner provocateur. Use only with the right persona and channel.

2. Build earns trust.
3. Bridge turns the provocation into a Folloze conversation.

## Voice

Folloze sounds like a direct, experienced peer who has been in the trenches with B2B marketers. The voice is confident without bravado, specific over abstract, and willing to name what is broken and what is coming.

Technical accuracy is non-negotiable. A bold claim fails if it gets the funnel wrong, compresses the buyer journey for drama, or sounds like the writer does not understand the tactic.

Use:

- Build, activate, signal.
- Activate, deploy, host, play, personalize.
- Govern, capture, route, surface.
- Precision, relevance, accuracy.
- Convert, land, move.
- Microsite, personalized account experience.
- Pipeline, engagement signal, first-party data.
- Speed of personalization.
- Marketers, frontline, demand gen, ABM.
- ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini.
- LLM, agent, prompt, MCP.
- In minutes, in hours, in real time, at scale.

Avoid:

- Empower, unlock, revolutionize, transform.
- Dive deep, unleash, supercharge, elevate.
- Game-changing, best-in-class, cutting-edge.
- Smart, intelligent, innovative, holistic.
- Set-it-and-forget-it, streamline, leverage.
- Seamless, next-generation.
- AI-powered as a standalone descriptor.
- Solution, synergy, comprehensive, robust.
- Journey and ecosystem when overused.
- Buyer Experience Platform, ABX, BXP.

Do not use em dashes.

## Content Brief Template

Every content request should capture:

- Content type.
- Primary persona.
- Lead hook.
- Funnel stage.
- Pain point.
- Industry or segment, when targeted.
- Named account, when 1:1.
- Mode of operation, when useful: LLM-centric, stack-driven, on-platform, or automated engine.
- Proof to use from the Customer Proof Library, or `[PROOF]` when not yet sourced.
- Proof pillars to activate.
- Agentic use case to reference, when useful.
- Tone intensity: high, mid, or low.
- Output variants.
- Additional context, constraints, competitor restrictions, and channel notes.
