# Campaign Brief Template

Use this template to turn a marketer's campaign idea into the minimum information needed to recommend a wireframe and build a Folloze-ready page.

## Brief Fields

```text
Campaign brief:
- Campaign name:
- Campaign goal:
- Audience:
- Campaign style:
- Primary action:
- Secondary actions:
- Offer or destination:
- Brand/source design:
- Campaign design context:
- Research and data inputs:
- Materials available:
- Buyer-safe proof:
- Strategy-only inputs:
- Anti-references:
- Required integrations or tracking:
- Review constraints:
- Missing inputs:
- Assumptions:
```

## Intake Guidance

Ask only for what is missing. Most marketers will arrive with a campaign idea, not a complete production spec.

Minimum questions when the brief is under-specified:

- What is the campaign goal?
- Who is the audience?
- What campaign style should this be?
- What sources, research, or data should inform it?
- What materials must be included?
- What action should the page drive?
- What brand page, screenshot, design system, or design context should it follow?
- What should the board avoid looking or sounding like?

If the marketer provides data-source names, record how each source should be used:

- buyer-facing copy
- proof or claims
- segmentation or personalization
- routing or CTA logic
- internal strategy only
- not approved for use

## Research Needed

Decide what research is needed based on the campaign style:

- ABM microsite: target-account context, account priorities, industry pressure, buyer roles, approved account proof, personalized CTA.
- Demand generation page: segment pain, offer value, conversion path, proof, form or meeting CTA, channel context.
- Event follow-up: event theme, attendee segment, session content, promised resources, next-step CTA, speaker or booth context.
- Product launch: launch message, new capability, target use case, proof, enablement assets, conversion path.
- Nurture or resource hub: content taxonomy, audience maturity, recommended paths, featured assets, progressive CTAs.
- Customer expansion: current customer context, adoption or value story, expansion motion, approved account-safe language, next meeting or workshop.
- Partner campaign: partner positioning, joint value proposition, co-branded assets, channel CTA, proof both parties approve.

## Materials Needed

Common materials:

- logo files or approved logo URLs
- brand guide, source website, or screenshots
- design system, design context file, or examples of approved campaign pages
- approved campaign copy or messaging
- product pages, one-pagers, decks, videos, webinars, or case studies
- offer details, form destination, registration link, meeting link, or resource links
- approved customer proof, quotes, metrics, analyst proof, or badges
- audience list, account list, personas, industries, or segments
- compliance, legal, accessibility, localization, or review instructions

If materials are missing, decide whether to proceed with labeled assumptions or stop for required assets.

## Design Context Inputs

Capture design context separately from research context.

Useful design inputs:

- source website or campaign page
- brand guide or design system
- approved screenshots
- customer-owned design context file
- examples of pages the customer likes
- examples of pages the customer does not want to resemble
- known typography, color, button, card, layout, and motion rules

If no design input is provided, ask for the best available source. Do not invent a brand system when a customer source can be supplied.

## Source Boundaries

Classify every input:

- Public and buyer-safe: can appear in visible copy.
- Customer-approved: can appear if used exactly or within the approved intent.
- Private strategy input: can inform the wireframe and message spine but cannot appear directly.
- Unverified: cannot be used as a factual claim.
- Prohibited: must not be used.

For private inputs, translate the intent into buyer-safe language. Do not expose internal scores, notes, contact behavior, pipeline stage, account surveillance, private meeting content, or raw intent signals unless the marketer explicitly approves that visible use.
