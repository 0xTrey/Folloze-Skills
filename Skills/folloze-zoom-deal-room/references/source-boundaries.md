# Source Boundaries

Use this reference before turning Zoom notes into visible page copy.

## Source Priority

1. Explicit user direction for this asset.
2. The Zoom recap, transcript, or meeting-assets email.
3. Salesforce account, contact, opportunity, and next-step context.
4. Related Gmail, Calendar, Granola, Drive, or Slack evidence.
5. Public account and product research.
6. `folloze-brand-kit` for Folloze value props, proof, and voice.

When sources conflict, keep the conflict in the internal brief. Do not smooth over account names, titles, stakeholders, dates, pricing, or next steps before a teammate sends or publishes the asset.

## Private Inputs

Treat these as internal strategy inputs:

- raw Zoom transcript or recap text
- buyer objections and budget constraints
- commercial package ideas and discount posture
- internal owner names and task routing
- Salesforce stage, amount, next step, MEDDPICC, or account notes
- email-thread state, who has or has not replied, and internal follow-up gaps
- call intelligence, intent signals, engagement metrics, and browsing behavior

Private inputs can shape the page strategy, section order, resource plan, and next recommended CTA. They should not appear verbatim in buyer-facing copy.

## Buyer-Safe Translation

Translate private notes into public-facing business language:

| Private input | Buyer-safe version |
| --- | --- |
| Budget is tight | Start with a focused launch path that proves value before expanding. |
| They use Luma | Support the event workflow they already use, then improve follow-up and measurement. |
| Need to bring in Iho and Alia | Give the broader team a single place to review examples, security material, and next steps. |
| Wants low 30s package | Present a focused package path without exposing internal commercial posture. |
| Need MSA and infosec | Add a procurement-readiness section with security and legal resources. |
| They liked the Claude build | Show how AI-created content becomes governed, hosted, personalized, and measurable. |

## External Copy Rules

- Do not say `Zoom recap`, `meeting notes`, `transcript`, `AI summary`, or `internal notes`.
- Do not expose pricing numbers unless the user explicitly approves the commercial language for the buyer.
- Do not expose named internal follow-up owners unless the owner is part of the customer-facing team and the page needs a contact card.
- Do not imply a customer said a quote unless there is an approved quote.
- Do not turn private objections into accusations. Reframe them as evaluation questions or proof needs.
- Use public account facts or user-approved language for company-specific claims.

## Internal Brief Rules

The internal brief may include private details, but label them clearly:

- `Buyer-safe`: can appear externally.
- `Internal only`: informs strategy but must not be visible.
- `Needs confirmation`: should block save or be phrased as an assumption.
- `Source conflict`: must be resolved before sending externally.
